Proposition 4.4.4. Iterative Criterion for Pairwise Relatively Prime Integers.

If \(a,b,c\) are positive integers, then they are pairwise relatively prime if and only if \(a\) and \(b\) are relatively prime, and \(ab\) and \(c\) are relatively prime
🔗
🔗

Hint.

Try a direct proof. First show the forward direction. If \(a,b,c\) are pairwise relatively prime, then \(\gcd(a,b)=1\) and \(\gcd(b,c)=1\text{.}\) Show that this implies \(\gcd(ab,c)=1\text{.}\) Now show the backward direction. If \(a\) and \(b\) are relatively prime, and \(ab\) and \(c\) are relatively prime, then show that \(\gcd(a,c)=1\) and \(\gcd(b,c)=1\text{.}\) You may find Proposition 3.2.2, Definition 2.2.2, and Definition 2.3.4 useful.
