Proposition 4.1.1. Multiplication by relatively prime element permutes the units.

If \(a, m \in \Z\text{,}\) \(m > 1\) is a positive integer, such that \(\gcd(a,m)=1\text{,}\) then multiplication by \(a\) permutes the elements of \(U_m\text{.}\) In other words, the function \(f: U_m \to U_m\) defined by \(f(x) = ax \pmod m\) is a bijection.

Hint.

Try a direct proof. First, if \(r\in U_m\text{,}\) then \(gcd(ar,m)=1\) (why???). Then, suppose that \(ar_1 \equiv ar_2 \pmod m\) for some \(r_1, r_2 \in U_m\text{.}\) Deduce that \(r_1 \equiv r_2 \pmod m\text{.}\) You may find Proposition 3.4.2, Definition 3.4.1, Definition 2.4.1, and Proposition 2.4.6 useful.
