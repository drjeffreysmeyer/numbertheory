Definition 3.3.1. The Relatively Prime Diagonal Grid.

If integers \(m\) and \(n\) are relatively prime and greater than 1, then the relatively prime diagonal grid is the \(m\) by \(n\) grid of least residues modulo \(mn\) written sequentially diagonally down and to the right, where you loop upon hitting and edge. In symbols, the least residue \(k\in\{0,1,\ldots,mn-1\}\) will be placed in row \(i\equiv k\pmod m\) and column \(j\equiv k\pmod n\text{.}\)
