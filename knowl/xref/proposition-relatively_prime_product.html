Proposition 3.4.6. Relatively prime with a relatively prime product.

If \(a,m,n\in \Z\) integers greater than 1 and \(m\) and \(n\) are relatively prime, then \(a\) is relatively prime with \(mn\) if and only if \(a\) is relatively prime with both \(m\) and \(n\text{.}\)

Hint.

Try a proof by contrapositive. First suppose that \(a\) and \(mn\) are not relatively prime. Then there exists a prime \(p\) such that \(p \mid a\) and \(p \mid mn\text{.}\) Deduce that either \(m\) or \(n\) is not relatively prime with \(a\text{.}\) Now prove the other direction. Without loss of generality, we may suppose (why???) that \(m\) and \(a\) are not relatively prime. Deduce \(a\) is not relatively prime with \(mn\text{.}\) You may find Definition 3.1.1, Definition 2.2.2, Definition 2.3.4, Proposition 3.1.5, and Proposition 3.2.2 useful.
